Some people say that the main environmental problem of our time is the loss of particular species of plants and animals. Other say that there are more import environmental problems.

發表於2023-10-21|更新於2023-11-01|English
|字數總計:288|閱讀時長:1分鐘|閱讀量:

Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

Environmental concerns have undeniably risen to the forefront of global issues in recent times. While some individuals emphasize the significance of the loss of specific species as the primary environmental challenge, others contend that there exist more critical problems warranting our immediate attention. In this essay, I will delve into both perspectives and present my own viewpoint.

Animals and plants play pivotal roles in our ecosystem. Their absence would lead to a loss of biodiversity, severely impacting the environment. Most plants are essential for carbon dioxide to oxygen conversion, a critical process for numerous Earth inhabitants. Moreover, certain animal species may become extinct due to the rising of sea levels, resulting in the destruction of its biodiversity, and thus, the disruption of food chains. Some fish or plants may disappear from our dining table and some farmers or fishermen may lose their livelihoods.

On the other hand, these environmental problems have direct and significant consequences for humans, making them more urgent and critical than the loss of biodiversity. First, air pollution has severe impacts on the individuals’ health, resulting in the disease or even cancer in cities such as Beijing. Second, rising sea levels affect island nations, forcing residents to leave their homes. Last but not least, improper disposal of plastic waste and trash not only fosters the proliferation of insects but makes our cities filthy or unsanitary.

In conclusion, though the loss of specific species is a significant environmental concern, issues such as air pollution, rising sea-level, abd improper waste disposal have more immediate and direct impacts on humanity. It is vital that we prioritize environmental protection to safeguard not only biodiversity but our own well-being.
